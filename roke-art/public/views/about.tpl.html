About

My name is Roke Muna and here’s hoping you enjoy the “browse” through some of my oil and acrylic paintings that I’ve completed during the past few years. I was born and raised in a small but beautiful western pacific island called Guam-in a tiny village of about 7500 people. My village is called Agat and I can honestly say that a lot of my work was influenced by living on the island. A lot of the themes I selected for my artwork were from old pictures of southern Guam. Many of these were photos taken while my family and I visited families and friends and the beautiful local sites all over Guam over these past thirty years.

Currently, I reside in Albuquerque with Dorothy, my wife, and you will see some of the work I’ve completed incorporating the Southwestern theme. We’ve raised three wonderful children and now they have beautiful children of their own. We are blessed! Mexico is a great place to paint and to learn about styles and genres as many notable painters and artists (Hurley, Gorman, O’Keeffe, Hartley, etc.) have embraced the States beauty, it’s mercurial skies, colorful mesas, and interesting people and applied their artistic skills to the canvases and other medium. I have only started painting within the past twelve years but started drawing with a pencil since the fourth grade.

I am still learning the trade, and will do so for years to come. I believe everyone is an artist to some degree, but some pursue it more than others. Dorothy has been my biggest art fan and advocate, especially when it comes to art themes, concepts, and words of encouragement. I could not have gone this far without her love and support!

My work is on permanent display at some of the local restaurants and clinics in Albuquerque. I’ve also done work for many families and friends. I hope to have my paintings on display someday in Guam (banks, Government offices, restaurants, etc.) and also in more places in New Mexico.

Thanks again for the visit and please call me if you have questions and art inquiries.

Si Yu’os Ma’ase,
Roke
